## Step 4 – Structural Analysis (IDT OligoAnalyzer)

### Forward Primer

Sequence:  
CTTTTGCGTCGCCAGCCGAG

#### Hairpin Analysis

The most stable predicted hairpin structure showed:

ΔG = -1.35 kcal/mol  
Tm = 47.6 °C

This value indicates a weak hairpin structure that is unlikely to interfere with PCR amplification.

#### Self-Dimer Analysis

The most stable self-dimer structure showed:

ΔG = -6.75 kcal/mol  
Base pairs = 3

This interaction is considered moderate but does not involve strong 3' complementarity, suggesting a low risk of primer-dimer formation during PCR.

#### Hetero-Dimer Analysis

Interaction analysis between the forward and reverse primers showed no strong complementarity at the 3′ ends.

The predicted heterodimer interactions were weak and are unlikely to interfere with PCR amplification.

Overall, the forward primer demonstrates acceptable structural stability for PCR applications.

### Reverse Primer Structural Analysis

The reverse primer was evaluated using IDT OligoAnalyzer for secondary structure formation.

#### Hairpin Analysis
Hairpin structures showed very weak stability, with ΔG values ranging from -0.68 to -0.02 kcal/mol, indicating negligible hairpin formation.

#### Self-Dimer Analysis
Although the maximum ΔG value reached -39.49 kcal/mol, the most stable dimer structures had ΔG values around -3.6 kcal/mol with only 2–3 base pairs, suggesting weak and non-problematic dimerization.

#### Hetero-Dimer Analysis
No significant complementarity was observed between the forward and reverse primers, particularly at the 3’ ends, indicating minimal risk of cross-dimer formation.

#### Conclusion
The reverse primer demonstrates acceptable structural stability, with no significant risk of hairpin formation, self-dimerization, or cross-dimerization. Therefore, it is suitable for PCR amplification.
